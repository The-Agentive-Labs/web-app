Discovery Environmental Analysis

STEEP/PESTLE Analysis

Assess macro-environmental factors that influence product strategy and user behavior.

Learn

What is STEEP/PESTLE?

STEEP and PESTLE are strategic frameworks for analyzing external macro-environmental factors that impact your product, market, or users. Both examine Social, Technological, Economic, Environmental/Ecological, and Political/Legal forces. PESTLE adds "Legal" as a distinct category. These frameworks help teams anticipate trends, risks, and opportunities outside their direct control.

The Five (or Six) Forces

  • Social: Demographics, cultural trends, lifestyle changes, values shifts
  • Technological: Innovation, automation, digital transformation, emerging tech
  • Economic: Market conditions, employment, inflation, purchasing power
  • Environmental/Ecological: Sustainability, climate impact, resource scarcity
  • Political: Government policy, regulations, trade agreements, political stability
  • Legal (PESTLE only): Laws, compliance, intellectual property, data privacy

Framework & Placement

STEEP/PESTLE is used during Discovery:

  • After: Problem hypothesis formation
  • Before: Primary research and detailed user studies

It provides strategic context for product planning and helps identify external risks and opportunities.

Best Practices

  • Focus on Relevance: Not every factor matters for every product. Prioritize forces with the highest impact on your users or business.
  • Look 3-5 Years Ahead: STEEP/PESTLE is about anticipating change, not reacting to today's news.
  • Use Data: Ground your analysis in reports, studies, and trends—not assumptions. Cite sources.
  • Identify Opportunities and Threats: For each factor, ask: "How does this create an opportunity?" and "What risks does this introduce?"
  • Connect to User Behavior: Macro trends only matter if they affect how users think, feel, or act. Draw clear links to user needs.
  • Revisit Regularly: Environmental factors change. Update your analysis annually or when major events occur.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Define Your Scope: Are you analyzing a specific market, user segment, or product category? Be clear on what you're assessing.
  2. Research Each Factor: Gather data from industry reports, government statistics, news, and trend forecasts. Look for credible, recent sources.
  3. Identify Key Trends: For each STEEP/PESTLE category, list 2-5 major trends relevant to your product/users.
  4. Assess Impact: How does each trend affect your users' behaviors, needs, or purchasing decisions? Be specific.
  5. Flag Opportunities: Where can your product align with or capitalize on these trends?
  6. Flag Threats: What risks or barriers do these trends introduce? What could derail your strategy?
  7. Prioritize: Not all factors are equally important. Rank by potential impact and urgency.

Example: Freelance Invoice App

Social:

  • Trend: 59 million Americans freelance (36% of workforce). Gen Z/Millennials value flexibility over traditional employment.
  • Impact: Growing market of freelancers needing financial tools designed for non-traditional work.
  • Opportunity: Position as the "finance OS for the freelance economy."

Technological:

  • Trend: AI-powered accounting tools, embedded payments (Stripe, PayPal APIs), mobile-first workflows.
  • Impact: Users expect automation, real-time syncing, and mobile access.
  • Threat: Competitors may out-innovate us with AI-powered features (e.g., automated expense categorization).

Economic:

  • Trend: Economic uncertainty increases freelancing (people seek side income). But recessions reduce client budgets.
  • Impact: More freelancers need invoicing tools, but they're price-sensitive.
  • Opportunity: Offer a freemium model to capture budget-conscious users.

Environmental:

  • Trend: Digital-first work reduces paper invoicing. Sustainability is a growing value for consumers.
  • Impact: Users prefer paperless, eco-friendly solutions.
  • Opportunity: Market as a sustainable alternative to traditional invoicing.

Political/Legal:

  • Trend: New gig economy regulations (e.g., California AB5, EU platform worker laws). Tax compliance complexity for freelancers.
  • Impact: Freelancers face increased legal/tax burdens.
  • Opportunity: Build compliance features (tax estimation, 1099 generation).
  • Threat: Regulations could reclassify freelancers as employees, shrinking our market.

AI + Human Collaboration

Human Value

  • Strategic judgment to assess which trends matter most
  • Contextual understanding of local/regional nuances
  • Critical thinking to connect macro trends to user behavior
  • Ability to anticipate second-order effects of trends

AI Value

  • Rapid scanning of reports, news, and trend forecasts
  • Extraction of key statistics and data points
  • Identification of emerging patterns across sources
  • Generation of trend summaries by category

Key Considerations

  • AI for Data Gathering: Use AI to scan industry reports and news for relevant trends. But humans must validate sources and assess credibility.
  • Trend Interpretation: AI can surface trends but struggles to assess strategic importance. Humans must prioritize based on business context.
  • Regional Nuances: AI may miss local/cultural factors. Humans must add context for specific markets.

"AI in the Loop" Model

  1. Trend Discovery: AI scans databases (Gartner, Statista, news) for STEEP/PESTLE factors. Humans curate the most relevant trends.
  2. Data Extraction: AI extracts key statistics and quotes from reports. Humans synthesize into actionable insights.
  3. Impact Assessment: Humans evaluate how each trend affects users and strategy. AI cross-references with competitor analyses.

Watch-Outs

  • Don't let AI generate the entire analysis. It lacks strategic judgment and business context.
  • Avoid echo chambers. AI may surface trends that confirm your existing beliefs. Seek contradictory perspectives.
  • Be cautious of AI overweighting sensational news. Focus on substantive, long-term trends, not headlines.
